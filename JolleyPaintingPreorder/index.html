Painting: Tayler Jolley with the Savior

This painting I made for the Jolley family is a very special one. Tayler Jolley died unexpectedly of a pulmonary embolism on August 7th 2024, leaving her family and everyone around her absolutely shattered. I was honored and deeply humbled to create this painting at their request- Tayler with the savior.
Looking at this painting helps me to know that Tayler is in a better place. I can feel that she’s safe, protected, and surrounded by love; clasped in the arms of Jesus. And making this painting taught me undeniably, powerfully, and unexpectedly; about the profound love of God surrounding her family in their grief.
When I accepted this commission, I was slightly terrified. I have skills in portraiture and it would seem like this would be right up my alley, but it didn’t feel that way to me. I’d been trying to paint the savior for a while beforehand, and I’d always felt it was about much more than paint on a canvas. It mattered to me deeply and I wanted to do it right. At times, the gravity of it made it hard to pick up the brush. I really tried. When this opportunity came I knew that my struggle had prepared me. And I knew that God wanted me to paint this painting, so I trusted he would help me figure it out. And he did.
I don’t think I can explain exactly what it was like to create. It took me over a year. I would estimate I spent about 150 hours off the canvas, 40 hours painting, and lots of time doing spiritual work and waiting for direction. I felt a lot of uncertainty and difficulty, and I wanted it to move along much faster than it did. I ended up buckling down in a lot of fasting and prayer, and lots of time in the temple. I noticed that many aspects of my life transformed throughout the process. I was working through the atonement of Christ! Only after I’d given my best- my very best- did he make up the rest. Because He wanted me to become something! And I look into that painting now with different eyes than the ones with which I began.
This painting only exists because of God’s love for the Jolley family. He expanded one of his small servants to deliver a message that is true. He’s caring for Dan and Lori’s girl now. And he is ever with them.
